This is a moon jellyfish

Its scientific name is Aurelia

The genus was first described by Jean-Baptiste Lamarck in 1816

It can grow up to 40 cm in diameter

Its appearance is characterised by four oval gonads in the centre of the bell

Moon Jellyfish

Aurelia

The translucent moon jellyfish is a common sight along the coastline of Hong Kong

Hello

Aurelia sp. regularly shows up in Hong Kong in July in large numbers and can be found in local waters through September. One of the earlier mentions of Aurelia in Hong Kong is from a PhD student who studied the effects of marine life on the water intake of a local power plant in the 1980s.

The species in Aurelia keep changing, and there is a lot of confusion about how many species are in the genus. Many species used to be lumped into Aurelia aurita, leaving the impression that it was a cosmopolitan species.

However, Aurelia was recently split into 27 different species. Many of these are not currently described. Currently we don’t know what species of Aurelia is in Hong Kong, so it is an exciting area of future research. Aurelia is considered one of the edible species of jellyfish.
